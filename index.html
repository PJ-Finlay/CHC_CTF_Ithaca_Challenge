All About Ithaca


Image Credit: Wikipedia User Attacker48

Ithaca is a city of 30,000 people located in the Finger Lakes Region of Upstate New York. It is home to both Cornell University and Ithaca College. Before European contact Ithaca was inhabited by the Cayuga Iroquois tribe. Currently Ithaca's economy primarily revolves around education, but previously Ithaca was a major manufacturer of chains and guns. Ithaca is also known for its natural beauty with a number of gorges and waterfalls, including Taughannock Falls which is the tallest free falling waterfall east of the Mississippi.
